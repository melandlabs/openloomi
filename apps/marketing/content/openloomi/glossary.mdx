# Glossary

This page defines the core terms used throughout OpenLoomi's documentation. These are the concepts that underpin how the product works.

---

## Event

An **Event** is the fundamental content unit in OpenLoomi. Every message from every connected platform is analyzed and grouped with related messages into a single Event — one card per real-world thing happening.

Events are:

- Automatically prioritized into **High**, **Medium**, and **Low** bands
- Contain an AI-generated description, all related messages, a timeline of how the event evolved, and the full message thread
- Repliable, annotatable, and explorable via OpenLoomi's Chat

See [Events](/docs/openloomi/understanding/events) for the full guide.

---

## Focus

**Focus** is one of the two main views in OpenLoomi's workspace. It surfaces what needs your immediate attention right now.

Events in the Focus view are ranked by priority: **High**, **Medium**, and **Low**.

See [Understanding](/docs/openloomi/understanding) for how Focus relates to the other views.

## Connectors

A **Connector** is an integration between OpenLoomi and an external platform (Slack, Telegram, WhatsApp, Gmail, etc.). Connectors are what allow OpenLoomi to read and interact with your messages across different services.

See [Connectors](/docs/openloomi/connectors) for the full list.

---

## Skills

**Skills** are specialized capabilities that OpenLoomi uses to accomplish specific tasks. OpenLoomi has access to 200+ skills and automatically selects the right ones based on what you ask. You can also manually select skills in the message composer.

See [Skills](/docs/openloomi/skills) for the full list.

---

## Automation

An **Automation** is a workflow you create to automate repetitive tasks — such as sending scheduled summaries, responding to specific triggers, or routing messages into your OpenLoomi workspace.

See [Automation](/docs/openloomi/automation) to get started.
