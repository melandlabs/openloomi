# Understanding

**Every day, hundreds of messages arrive across Slack, Telegram, WhatsApp, Gmail, and more. Most of it is noise. Some of it matters — a lot.**

Alloomi reads across all your connected platforms, figures out what actually deserves your attention, and surfaces it in one structured workspace. Instead of context-switching between apps, you get a single place where the important stuff is already found, grouped, and ready to act on.

> **Tip:** If you're new to Alloomi, the [Glossary](/docs/alloomi/glossary) defines all core terms in one place.

---

## Three things that make this work

### [Events](/docs/alloomi/understanding/events)

Alloomi doesn't just forward messages. It reads conversations, identifies what they're about, and groups related messages into a single **Event** — one card per real-world thing happening.

Your Focus view shows these events ranked by priority: **High**, **Medium**, and **Low**. You see what needs attention now, not a flood of unread counts.

Inside each event, you can read the full thread, ask Alloomi questions about it, reply directly to the original platform, and attach notes or files — all without leaving Alloomi.

→ **[Learn how Events work](/docs/alloomi/understanding/events)**

## How it all fits together

```
Connected platforms (Slack, Telegram, Gmail, WhatsApp, ...)
        ↓
  Alloomi reads and groups into Events
        ↓
  Focus → ranked by priority (High / Medium / Low)
  Tracking → organized by your Context tabs
        ↓
  Interests tune both views toward what you care about
```

The more you use Alloomi — following people, adding topics, replying to events — the better it gets at knowing what deserves your attention.
